Comparison

High Output of Raw Material?

More production doesn't always mean more revenue — Oregon's oversupply crisis proves it.

Missouri

High Output — Healthy Business Growth
  • 350,000 – 450,000 lbs of cannabis grown in 2025
  • $1.57 billion in yearly revenue ($4.23M per day)
  • 20,100 full-time jobs

Oregon

Extremely High Output — Crisis: Over-Producing
  • 13.5 million lbs grown in 2025 (35× higher than MO)
  • $920 million in yearly revenue (less than MO)
  • Low prices due to oversupply and low barrier to entry
Key Insight: Oregon produces 35× more cannabis than Missouri yet earns less revenue. Prices have collapsed due to heavy competition and a low barrier to entry.
Comparison

Missouri vs. Oregon — Key Differences

Missouri

  • Tax Rate: Low (6%)
  • Market Stage: Growth (rec started 2023)
  • Supply vs. Demand: Demand > Supply
  • Pricing: Stable
  • License Access: Open
  • Outcome: Higher margins

Oregon

  • Tax Rate: High (17%)
  • Market Stage: Mature / Saturated
  • Supply vs. Demand: Supply >> Demand
  • Pricing: Collapsed ($3.33/gram flower)
  • License Access: Moratorium
  • Outcome: Efficiency or exit

Summary

Business Impact Summary

Missouri

  • Low taxes means higher profit margins
  • Rapid demand growth allows producers to scale their supply chain quickly
  • Opportunity: Vertical integration and early expansion

Oregon

  • High taxes coupled with oversupply leads to thinner margins
  • Focus on branded products (vapes, edibles, concentrates)
  • Survival strategy: Cost efficiency and differentiation
Takeaways

Two Key Takeaways

1

Low tax, recently legalized markets (like Missouri) offer the best near-term ROI. Profits are higher, and scaling supply chain quickly captures demand spikes.

2

Market maturity decides the strategy:

  • Mature (Oregon): Compete on efficiency and manufactured goods
  • Growth (Missouri): Compete on capacity and vertical integration
